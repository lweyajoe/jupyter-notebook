1. Financial Model For A Fashion Line (E-Commerce)

Description

Squarehaul is not the real name, but a dummy name for a real client's company. Squarehaul wanted a model to test the viability of a new business line. This business line is a fashion line with 5 products; Product A, Product B, Product C, Product D and Product E. The products are actually cloth items. The very basis of the model is a share of the Total Addressable Market that is to be captured by Squarehaul on a 5-year period. The first input variable here is therefore the population of the locality targeted by the client.


This model was to serve 3 purposes;

  1. Project and visualize financials (revenue, profits etc)
  2. Project and visualize KPIs against an industry benchmark as directed by the client; Gross margin percentage, Profit margin percentage, Wages as percentage of revenue, Average weekly revenue, Average weekly net profit
  3. Capture sources and sizes of funding and the valuation analysis

The assignment was to create the model and prepare a business case on the various methods of sales to be used by the client ie: buy-and-stock, dropship, order-and-customise. You can play around with the model (inputs are cells in yellow) or sample the dashboards in the various sheets below. 2. Mortgage Amortization Calculator For Financial Advisors - Bank

Description

This is another project I've handled.

The client, an 'off-plan-sales housing developer' who sells houses on cash basis, rent-to-own basis and/or mortgage basis wanted a simple calculator that financial advisors and wealth managers would use to demonstrate to the company's clients how their loan ages with time when they buy houses using the chosen modes. This was a simple project. The calculator was eventually embeded on the client's website. The inputs are cells in blue. The loan amount, term and lender's rate are the most important inputs. The outputs are the monthly payments and the life of the loan.
